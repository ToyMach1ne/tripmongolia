    Taikhar chuluu

    • March 15, 2016
    • admin
    • Central Mongolia, Destinations

    Taikhar Rock is located 22 kilometres (14 miles) northeast from Tsetserleg, in the village of Ikh Tamir, south the Khoid Tamir River. It’s a big 20-metres (66 feet) high granite rock, covered with 150 writings in many different languages and dating back from many different periods (runic, Sogd, Uyghur, Mongolian, Nangiad, Tibetan writings). The oldest one is the runic writing, dating back from the turkish period of the 6th and 7th centuries. An ovoowas placed at its top. Unfortunately, there are many graffiti on this rock and the old writings almost disappeared. The government decided to protect the rock in 1994.

    Khoton Lake

    • March 13, 2016
    • admin
    • Destinations, Western Mongolia

    Khoton Lake is gorgeous alpine lake, located in Bayan-Ulgii province. It is fresh water lake nestled in a mountain depression surrounded by high mountains with forests. A large numbers of beautiful gulls and migrant birds live at the Khoton lake in summer. It has a maximum depth of 8.58m.

    Egiin gol river

    • March 13, 2016
    • admin
    • Destinations, Northern Mongolia

    Egiin gol river as well as tributaries are populated with Siberian grayling (about 40 cm) and lenok (50-65 cm), Taimen population is abobe-average here, and due to this there is a big chance to catch trophy pieces over 130cm. Egiin gol river is also quite suitable for fly fishing. The peaceful environment offers a perfect opportunity to escape the tension of the city and just relax and unwind.

    Malchin Peak

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Destinations, Western Mongolia

    Malchin Peak is one of five peaks of the Tavan Bogd mountain on the Mongolia-Russian border. The peak towers at the elevation of 4,050m. It is only peak that can be climbed without specialized mountain climbing equipment.

    Erdenet City

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Destinations, Northern Mongolia

    Mongolia’s third largest city Erdenet is situated 370 kms north east of Ulaanbaatar. With a population of 120,000 people, Erdenet is one of the major industrial centers of the country and capital of Orkhon Province. This modern city was established in 1973, when Mongolian and Russian miners began to extract copper from the by-now huge mine 9 kms from the city center.

    Uushig Deer Stones

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Destinations, Northern Mongolia

    A deer-decorated stones in Uushigiin uver of Khuvsgul province considered as most clearly depicted deer stones in Mongolia. These deer stones were aligned from north to south in a single column. Each stone is 3-20m distant from each other and made of tetrahedral granite stone in reddish color. A deer, an arch, a shield, a horse, a knife, the moon, the sun and a mirror figured belt were engraved on the stones.

    Ugii Lake

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Central Mongolia, Destinations, M-101

    Ugii Lake is a freshwater lake in eastern Arkhangai province, in Central Mongolia. It engages 25sq.km of area, length is 7km, width is 5km. It is located 1337 meters above sea level and at the deepest part it reaches up to 15 meters. The lake, designated as a Ramsar Site of International Importance, is known for its fish and for birdlife. 14 kinds of fish, such as a perch, a luce and about 150 types of aquatic birds gather at Ugii lake. 50-80 tons of fish are caught annually. Its very convenient to have boat adventure here.

    Tuvkhun Monastery

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Central Mongolia, Destinations, M-101

    Tuvkhun monastery is another spectacular monastery. It is located in the high mountains marking the north side of Orkhon Valley at 2600m above sea level. One of Mongolia’s most remarkable and versatile figures, Zanabazar built this monastery in 1653. He lived, worked and meditated here for 30 years. Here he invented the Soyombo script in 1686 and madke his foamous creations as well.  Likewise other monasteries in the country, it was almost totally destroyed by the Communists. Today, there are special formation of rocks which are called womb and cradle, symbolizing rebirth, besides 4 temples and 2 stupas.

    Tsenkher Hot Spa

    • March 12, 2016
    • admin
    • Central Mongolia, Destinations

    Tsenkher is hot spring, which emerges from the ground at 85 Celsius degree. It lies south of Tsetserleg town in Arkhangai province. The waters of the spring contain hydrogen sulfide and considered as having curative properties for joints and other ailments. It is an excellent place to relax.
